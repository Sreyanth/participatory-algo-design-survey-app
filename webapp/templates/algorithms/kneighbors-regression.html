How does it work?

This algorithm predicts a student's score by taking an average of the scores of other students who have similar characteristics.

Let's try an example

To illustrate how it works, let's take the example of predicting the sale price of a house listing.

This algorithm works similarly to a real estate agent who assesses your house and suggests a listing price. You first tell the algorithm/agent all the features that you think should be associated with the sales price, such as the location, age, size, the surrounding amenities of the house, and so on. The agent will identify comparable homes in the same or similar neighborhoods with similar age, area, and other features to the place you are selling. A reasonable prediction for the price of your house should then be the average of the prices of these similar houses. Like the real estate agent, this algorithm identifies houses similar to a given house and predicts that the house's sale price is an average of the sale prices of all those similar houses.

Just like the real estate agent, this algorithm, identifies houses that are similar to a given house, and predicts that the sale price of the house is an average of the price of all those similar houses.

Advantages

The algorithm can easily explain its predictions by referring to outcomes of similar houses. It can also capture the more complicated relationships between the factors and outcomes than linear regression algorithms.

Disadvantages

The main drawback of this algorithm is that if we have to predict the price for a house like no other, it will be challenging because there are no other "neighbors" that are similar to the house. As a result, it would make a subpar prediction for this outlier.
